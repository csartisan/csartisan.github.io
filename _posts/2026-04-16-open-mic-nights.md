“Listen, and you will realize that we are not made from cells or from atoms. We are made from stories,” writer Mia Couto once wrote. This was never clearer for people attending  CSArts’ Creative Writing “Open- Mic Night.”  These nights, which are held throughout the school year, allow students a chance to present their original work. Although hosted by creative writing, all are welcome to everyone free of charge.

After attending two of them, creative writing student Alison Declerk said it was a “very fun, very nurturing environment …I always enjoy my time surrounded by other amazing writers and storytellers.”

Ella Bernthal, who read a short personal narrative at the last open mic, believes every story shows a piece of its author. She thinks open mic nights give students a safe space to share stories and build connections. Alison says there is definitely importance in “putting yourself out there.” 

Overall, open mic nights have proven to be a fun, rewarding experience to those there. The next “Open-Mic Night” is coming up on Tuesday, May 23, 2026, so keep an eye out on your emails where more information will be sent in advance.
